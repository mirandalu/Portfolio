The Challenge

The current cloud storage marketplace is packed with a wide variety of applications. However, users have encountered various challenges with the cloud storage services such as cluttered design, complex interface, and storage limitations.

Solution

Results from user research, discovery and competitive analysis showed that Easebox is the solution that places an emphasis on a simple, easy-to-use interface. Users are given the tools to create document, organize files, collaborate in real-time as well as access everything across all devices, making their workflow more streamlined and efficient.

Discovery Phase

User Research

I began my research by creating a user survey and distributed it to multiple social media platforms. I wanted to gain a better understanding of how potential users were using cloud storage services, as well as what they did and did not like about using these services.

What is your main reason for using Cloud Storage?
Which cloud storage services do you use?
Key Takeaways:
  • 80% of participants typically store, create, edit and collaborate on files
  • 68% of participants favor automatic file synchronization
  • 65% of participants find it helpful for cloud storage services to be accessible across devices
  • Participants would like to see an improvement on the ease of use of cloud storage services
  • Participants were most frustrated with price and storage space limitations

Competitive Analysis

Following the survey results, I took a close look at three market competitors that catered to specific interests: Google Drive, DropBox, and Pinterest. I studied each competitor’s positioning, primary audience, and key differentiators through a SWOT analysis. In analyzing the results from my survey and market competitors, I was able to determine the opportunities for Easebox:

  • Multi-device accessibility
  • Easy uploading and sharing process
  • Simple collaboration tools
  • Generous storage space at lower pricing tiers

Google Drive

  • Large amount of free storage
  • Can upload any type of file
  • Unoptimized organization
  • Requires users to have a gmail account

Dropbox

  • Intuitive navigation
  • Available on multiple platforms
  • Security of users data
  • Small amount of free storage
  • Can become expensive

Pinterest

  • Free and easy to use
  • Visually focused interface
  • Content limited to images, videos, and gifs
  • Lacks collaboration tools

Usability Tests

With the digital wireframes completed, I was able to conduct both remote and in-person user testing. To validate general user flows, I asked participants to complete the following tasks:


  • 1. Sign up for a new account
  • 2. Create a new content
  • 3. Move a content into a folder

While the participants were able to complete most tasks successfully, I learned through observations that various elements needed to be addressed on the dashboard.


Here were my top findings
  • Buttons placement was a concern for action clarity and accessibility
  • Users wanted to see files that are shared with other users
  • Users wanted a search box on the dashboard
  • Users suggested adding a dark overlay to selected items
  • Users assumed a “drag and drop” option for adding a file into a folder

Branding Identity

Branding Design

My next step in the process was to establish the visual aspect of the brand: logo, typography, and color scheme. I started with a mind mapping exercise to capture the brand value and gather ideas through word association. The entire brand was built with simplicity and the ease of service in mind. Hence the name Easebox suited the product.

Reflection

Navigating through my first project was an invaluable journey for me. I gained more insights on user research and testing to better back up my design decisions in explaining my mockups.
